Natural Bridge

University of Kentucky Geography

Natural Bridge is a natural sandstone arch located in the Natural Bridge State Resort Park within Powell and Wolfe Counties, Kentucky. The arch was formed over millions of years by the process of weather, and is estimated to be a million years old in its current condition. According to the State Parks website, Natural Bridge is composed of what is known in geological terms as Pottsville conglomeratic sandstone. Falling rocks on the sides originally started the formation of the arch.

Natural Bridge is 78 feet long, 65 feet tall, 12 feet thick, and 20 feet wide. The arch is the main attraction of the surrounding park, which is nearly 2,300 acres. It includes 18 miles of hiking trails and many scenic views. The area surrounding Natural Bridge is home to several rare species as well. Some species of animals include the Virginia Big-Eared Bat and Sharp-Shinned Hawk as well as some rare plant species.
